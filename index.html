Take Action

Guidelines for dealing with invasive plants

Fighting Invasives on Your Property

Step-by-step guide for safe removal of invasive plants

1

Identification

Accurately identify the species using our identifier tool.

2

Preparation

Put on protective clothing. For Giant Hogweed, a full protective suit is required.

3

Removal

Choose a method: mechanical, chemical, or biological control.

4

Disposal

Dry the plants or dispose according to local regulations. Never add them to compost.

5

Monitoring

Regularly inspect the area for 3-5 years after removal.

Control Methods

Mechanical

Digging out plants with roots using shovels and rakes.

Chemical

Applying herbicides strictly according to manufacturer instructions and local regulations.

Biological

Planting competitive native species or establishing ground cover.

Check your national or state invasive species reporting portal

How to make an effective report

  1. Take clear photos of the plant from multiple angles
  2. Record the exact location (GPS coordinates or address)
  3. Note the approximate number of plants
  4. Describe the size and condition of the plants
  5. Provide your contact information for follow-up

Finding Invasives in Natural Areas

What to do when you discover invasive plants in parks and nature reserves

Important Warning

Do not attempt to remove plants in natural areas on your own. This may disrupt the ecosystem and may be illegal.

Recommended steps

  1. Photograph the plant, including surrounding landmarks for location reference.
  2. Identify the species using our identifier tool.
  3. Report the finding to the park or reserve administration.
  4. Record precise GPS coordinates using your phone's location services.
  5. Submit your observation to iNaturalist or a similar citizen science platform.

About This Project

Open source invasive plant atlas built with care

Green Threat

Interactive Atlas of Invasive Plants

An open-source web application for identifying, learning about, and taking action against invasive plant species worldwide.

View Source Code Star on GitHub

Tech Stack

HTML5 CSS3 Vanilla JS JSON GSAP Google Fonts

Built with pure web technologies. No frameworks or build tools.

Features

  • Plant catalog with search and filters
  • Step-by-step identifier wizard
  • Detailed species information modals
  • Light and dark theme support
  • Fully responsive design
  • Action guides for different scenarios

How It Was Built

Single-page application built from scratch.

  • SPA-style routing without a router
  • Dynamic rendering from JSON data
  • CSS custom properties for theming
  • Scoring-based identification algorithm
  • Local storage for theme persistence
